title: "FAQ"
description: "Common questions about how Sparqo works."
---

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Does Sparqo post to Reddit automatically?">
    No. Posting to Reddit is manual by design. Automated API posting gets accounts banned, so Sparqo drafts the reply and you post it yourself from your own account. This protects your account.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do I need my own AI or OpenAI key?">
    No. Sparqo runs on a managed model out of the box. You connect your own tools (CMS, Search Console, analytics) when you want an agent to act, but you do not need to bring an AI key to start.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Where do the keywords and search volumes come from?">
    Real search data. Sparqo pulls live monthly volume, difficulty, competition and 12 month trends, and ranks the winnable ones (good demand, beatable difficulty) so you are not guessing.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What can I publish to?">
    Connect a CMS and publish with one click, or schedule a live auto-publish. Sparqo supports connectors like Strapi and a GitHub target for code or vibe-coded blogs. See [Connections](/guides/connections).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How is my workspace kept separate?">
    Every workspace is isolated. Your brand brain, drafts, keywords and connections are scoped to your workspace and never mixed with anyone else's.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How long until I see value?">
    You see the product working in minutes: winnable keywords with real data, drafts ready to write, and targeted subreddits, right after onboarding. Ranking and citations compound over weeks, but the work the agents do is visible immediately.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if my site is a JavaScript-only app?">
    Sparqo will tell you. A site that renders only after JavaScript runs is invisible to search and AI crawlers, so they cannot index or cite you. The fix is server-side rendering or prerendering. See [AI Visibility](/guides/ai-visibility).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
